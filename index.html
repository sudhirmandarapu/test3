Projects

Below are some of the projects I have worked on over the course of my undergraduate and graduate career:

System-Scale Armor Model

I am leading the modelling team in our research group to develop a state-of-the-art system-scale armor model project which is in collaboration with General Dynamics Land System, NP Aerospace, and Defence Research and Development Canada. This model significantly improves existing models from the literature by: (1) incorporating a fragmentation model for the long rod, (2) automating the geoemetry of the ceramic tiles to take into account the manufacturing defects (e.g., tolerances) when assembling the system (via Python scripting embedded in Blender), (3) and implementing a Johnson-Holmquist-Beissel model for the ceramic tiles via a finite element model to smoothed-particle hydrodyamics coupling. Furthermore, this project will employ machine learning techniques to perform uncertainty quantification analysis.

Adhesive Model for Armor Vehicles (click to view the research overview)

This project is in collaboration with General Dynamics Land System, NP Aerospace, and Defence Research and Development Canada. The objective of this project is to develop a 3-dimensional numerical model in LS-DYNAÂ® V11.1 to simulate dynamic failure and fracture in adhesives used in hybrid armor systems for land vehicles under ballistic impact. This research seeks to inform design decisions for (1) adhesive thickness, (2) manufacturing defects, and (3) material properties.

Body Armor Design (click to see a poster of the design)

This project was in collaboration with a local armor manufacturer, Milburn Mountain Defence, and Defence Research and Development Canada. The objective of this project was to design and fabricate drawings for a ballistic level III body armor and platens for a hydraulic press using CATIA and SolidWorks. The platens are used to manufacture the body armor.

Fuel Rail for a Heavy Duty Semi (click to see a poster of the design)

This Capstone project was in close collaboration with Innovative Fuel Systems. Here, I led a team of five students to design a compressed natural gas (CNG) fuel rail. The design is fitted in a converted dual-fuel semi truck delivers where the fuel rail delivers CNG from the fuel supply line to the six injectors.

Synthetic Microstructure Project

This project was in collaboration with the US Army Research Laboratory and Defence Research and Development Canada. The purpose of this project was to explore the characterization of secondary phase particles in materials (e.g., reinforcement fibers in composites and porous defects in ceramics) using MATLAB. 3D microstructures were simulated containing a variety of secondary phase particle spatial distributions and sensitivity of 3D characterization metrics were tested.

Bone Anchored Hearing Aid Dynamic Finite Element Model

This project was in collaboration with Dr. Lindsey Westover's group from the University of Alberta. The main objective of the project was to construct a numerical model of a bone conduction hearing aid implant-abutment system. This model represents a non-invasive test procedure known as the Advanced System for Implant Stability Testing (ASIST) which generates the acceleration response of the bone-implant interface. This non-invasive and quantitative procedure analyzes the structural integrity and stiffness of bone anchored hearing aid implants.
